music

Like Bing Crosby and Frank Sinatra before him, Elvis Presley became the newest heartthrob at the microphone. This time, however, the teen idol brought a new form of music to the mainstream - rock and roll.

By the early 1960s this new form of music had started to get watered down. The rawness and rough edges were gone, and rock and roll seemed destined for blandness.

A second era in rock music began with the arrival of the Fab Four. The Beatles would breathe new life into rock roll and become the most successful and influential band of all time.

By the late 1960s, rock music started to get adventurous. Even the Monkees were releasing psychedelic music, and The Beatles made the LP into an art form with Revolver and Sgt. Pepper.

In the early 1970s, there was a reaction against the over-the-top excesses of psychedelic music. The singer-songwriter movement was psychedelic musics polar opposite, and often nothing more than a musician and his or her acoustic guitar.

In the early 1970s, bands like Led Zeppelin and Grand Funk Railroad initiated a form of rock devised specifically for large concert venues. By mid-decade, stadium or arena rock was in full swing, and bands with big sounds like Boston and Queen dominated the music scene until the 1980s.

Punk rock was largely a reaction against stadium filling bands like Pink Floyd and other so-called pretentious rock acts. Punk songs were short and raw, and would never dream of containing long guitar solos or complex themed albums like their arena rock enemies.

Punk rock was, by its very nature, destined to self-destruct. Many punk bands like Blondie began to morph into a friendlier brand of music called New Wave. This shift coincided with a new television channel called MTV that suited their aristic style perfectly.

Heavy metal had its origins with Black Sabbath back in 1970, but it was this bands 1980 heavy metal masterpiece, British Steel, that sent metal on its path to world domination. By the latter half of the 1980s, bands like Ratt, Def Leppard, and Motley Crue would emerge that were perfectly suited for the excesses that characterized the 80s.
